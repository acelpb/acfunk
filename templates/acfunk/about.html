Anne-Christine Funk is a Belgo-German artist who grew up in the meeting place of the borders of Belgium, Germany, and the Netherlands, known as the Three-Country Point. She studied German and Russian literature, philosophy and art at the Free University of Brussels where she currently lives and works.

I discovered the Art of Drawing with Oil-pastels at a very early age and still use them almost exclusively today. They are a unique medium for creating colorful paintings and can be used for precise definitition when it comes to geometric shaping.

I started during my studies exploring in an symbolic way literary pieces and philosophical concepts such as Hermann Hesse’s « Steppenwolf » or Fink’s « Fensterhaftigkeit ». Through the precise rendering of geometric shapes combined with bright colors I intended to catch theoretical and complex thoughts into colorful pieces of art that would intrigue the spectator and thus bring him closer to what’s so often invisible and hidden in books.

By extending abstract thoughts into paintings I have recently come to study another form of abstraction : the concept of loneliness in our modern society. Living in a big city has brought me to depict places and buildings that are crowded and surrounded by people passing by, individuals that are overly interconnected through the internet and social media but yet are visibly lonely, « Zwischenräume » as defined by Peter Handke where we feel connected but still are complete strangers to each other. The aim is not to find artistic beauty in loneliness but to intercept that modern loneliness surrounding us in order to provide a different perspective on who we are and where we live.
