Anne-Christine Funk is a Belgo-German artist who grew up in the meeting place of the borders of Belgium, Germany, and the Netherlands, known as the Three-Country Point. She studied German and Russian literature, philosophy and art at the Free University of Brussels where she currently lives and works.

Her pastel art is an exploration of her world of thoughts reflecting in an abstract and symbolic way literary myth and modern landscapes.

Her more recent work has been studying the architectural beauty of Brussels streets, parcs and landscapes. The cityâ€™s architecture inspires her work to find artistry and harmony by transforming the urban landscapes into the expression of her dreams.

Her art is not a mimetic representation of the visible world but a subjective interpretation of it: the abstract rendering of geometric shapes combined with a surrealistic dimension expand the perceptible limits of nature and it surroundings. The assemblage of different kind of shapes cover and surround at the time the image they are catching.

Through her world of thoughts she defines in her paintings both the visible and the invisible in order to depict the beauty lying beneath the curtain of semblance.
